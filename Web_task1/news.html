23/10/2020

"We want to perform as we did in El Clásico and take the points", added the boss.

Zinedine Zidane took to the media room at Borussia-Park before the final training session ahead of the team's Champions League clash against Borussia Mönchengladbach, with the madridista coach previewing the game in the following terms: "We're pleased with how we performed in El Clásico and are happy with how we did. We put in a good team performance for the 90 minutes and we know that we face a tough side tomorrow who are capable of causing you problems at any time. That's why we'll have to stay strong for the whole 90 minutes to try and reproduce our performance from the weekend and take the points". "The performance we delivered in El Clásico is exactly the kind of display we need to produce tomorrow. We played well, fought as a team and the most important thing is that we try and do that tomorrow. We've got to reproduce what we did at the weekend. We've got to put in a good performance and we know the situation we're in this, our second Champions League game. We'll have to stay focused for the 90 minutes, fight and battle. It'll be tough and we'll have to be focused on what we do out on the pitch".

23/10/2020

After victory in El Clásico, Zidane's charges travel to Germany on Matchday 2 of the group phase (9pm CET).

Real Madrid tonight face the first away outing of the Champions League campaign at Borussia-Park (9pm CET), where they take on Borussia Mönchengladbach on Matchday 2 of the group stage. Zinedine Zidane's players will be looking to get the better of opponents that hold fond memories for madridista followers. Juanito's jumps of joy are the most memorable image of the iconic meeting between the two sides after he played his part in completing one of Real Madrid's most stunning European comebacks. Thirty-five years on from that match, the madridistas renew acquaintances with the German outfit and will be bidding to reproduce the performance that secured victory in El Clásico.

We never give up and we saw that on Saturday. Whenever we lose two games, we try to turn things around", explained the German midfielder.

Before taking to the Borussia-Park pitch for the team's training session, Toni Kroos addressed the media in a press conference in which he preview the side's second Champions League outing of the campaign: "We're aware of the significance of the game. After the defeat in our first match, against Shakhtar Donetsk, the pressure is on us to win, but I have faith in us. It's like a cup final for us". "I don't think that Zinedine Zidane has ever had any question marks about us. We all win and lose together. It's not a case of us not backing him when we lose. That'd be the easy thing to do, if things go badly, for us to speak badly of others. We're not like that and you saw that on Saturday". Return to Germany "I always enjoy coming back to Germany because it's my country, my language and everything's more straightforward for me. But I've always said that I enjoy myself at Madrid and like living in Madrid. Over the course of six years, you always receive offers, but I feel really at home in Madrid and my idea is to end my career here".
